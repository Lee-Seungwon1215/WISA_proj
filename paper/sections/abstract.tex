Constant-time (CT) discipline is the standard defense against timing side
channels, but post-quantum cryptography (PQC) stresses any single screening
technique. Memcheck-style taint checks detect secret-dependent branches and
memory addresses, yet pass cleanly over the KyberSlash class of leaks, where a
secret-derived integer division leaks through variable-latency instructions.
Structural verdicts can also depend on compiler and optimization level, while
stacking more checks increases the risk of overstating ambiguous findings. We
present CT-KAT, a fail-closed screening
workflow that binds Memcheck, a warn-only variable-latency assembly scan, and a
dudect-style timing test under one YAML configuration and a compiler-by-cflags
build matrix. Its registry-backed triage policy records build provenance and
keeps unrecognized or imprecisely attributed findings at \emph{needs-analysis}
until a reviewer rules. On PQClean ML-KEM/ML-DSA targets plus synthetic positive
controls, CT-KAT exposes a Memcheck-blind KyberSlash division, build-sensitive
structural verdicts, timing-only leakage controls, and an ML-DSA attribution
case where optimized builds surface coarser public/parent frames. The result is
a reproducible artifact for conservative CT screening: it surfaces candidates,
provenance, and review obligations without converting ambiguous evidence into
leak claims.
