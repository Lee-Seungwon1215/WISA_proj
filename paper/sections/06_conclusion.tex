\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

CT-KAT turns separate CT checks into reproducible, build-indexed screening
reports for PQC. A Valgrind/Memcheck structural check~\cite{valgrind,ctgrind}, a
warn-only variable-latency asm-scan, and dudect-style timing~\cite{dudect} share
one YAML config and feed a default-deny triage policy with a cited
accepted-variable-time registry. The corpus shows why that integration matters:
KyberSlash~\cite{kyberslash} passes structural checking but surfaces as a
secret-derived division candidate, synthetic controls exercise build-sensitive
and timing-only cases, and ML-DSA-65~\cite{fips204} demonstrates the conservative
stop condition when optimized builds blur attribution. Future work broadens the
PQC corpus, adds native timing confirmation, and resolves ML-DSA attribution via
dedicated no-inline builds.
